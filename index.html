David Gilmour

The Voice and Guitar of Pink Floyd

A short Biography

  • 6th March, 1946: Born in Cambridge, England
  • 1968: David Gilmour replaces Pink Floyd's original leader, Syd Barrett, due to his erratic behavipur. The band begins to move from psychedelic to progressive with the release of A Saucerful Of Secrets.
  • 1969: More released. This is Pink Floyd's first full album without Syd Barrett doubles as a soundtrack album to a little-seen movie by director Barbet Schroeder
  • 1969: 'Ummagumma' released
  • 1970: 'Atom Heart Mother' released
  • 1971: 'Meddle' released
  • 1972: 'Obscured by Clouds' released
  • 1973:'The Dark Side of the Moon' released
  • 1975: 'Wish You Were Here' released
  • 1977: 'Animals' released
  • 1979: 'The Wall' released
  • 1978: In 1978, David released his first solo album, David Gilmour, which charted in the UK and the US.
  • 1977: 'The Final Cut' released
  • 1977: David assumed control of Pink Floyd in 1985, after Roger Waters' departure, creating the new Floyd album A Momentary Lapse Of Reason with Richard Wright and Nick Mason.
  • 1994: 'The Division Bell' released. This album contained the instrumental Marooned, composed by David and Richard Wright, for which Pink Floyd won their sole Grammy Award.
  • 2006: David released his third solo album, On An Island, which entered the UK Charts at Number 1, subsequently hitting the top position in the pan-European Charts, as well as hitting multi-Platinum around the world.
  • 2009: David was awarded an Honorary Doctorate of Arts from East Anglia's Ruskin University of Cambridge and Chelmsford for his outstanding contribution to music as a writer, performer and innovator
  • 2014: In September 2014 Pink Floyd released The Endless River, and David confirmed that it would be the band's final album: it topped the charts in over 20 countries.
